Mapping Asian Hate

Applying data viz techniques to convey the discrepancy between anti-Asian hate crimes and media coverage.

üôÇ overview

In the Bay Area Immersion Program, I had the opportunity to take a class by Simon Rogers. The class introduced different data visualization techniques used in the industry and provided us with hands-on practice. For our final project, Simon wanted us choose a story idea that we could interpret well with data.

As many of my group members were Asian, we quickly honed in on a project that would look at the rise of anti-Asian hate crimes due to the COVID pandemic. We thought the numbers (and perhaps lack thereof) would tell a intriguing story that we were curious to uncover.

üôÇ process

Our first order of business was to locate and decide on datasets that we could use for our project. We soon began to realize that data on reported hate crimes were quite minimal, and the government only shared information from the year prior. Following, due to cultural differences, many victims tend to not report their hate crimes with local government agencies.

With my journalism background, I suggested we angle our story to focus on media coverage in regards to hate crimes in order for us to use published news stories as a potential dataset. Simon was a huge help in providing us with a Google News dataset that aggregated all news reports on hate crimes from 2021. The entire dataset had over 7,000 entries, so we split it amongst the five of us and narrowed down the dataset to articles on anti-Asian hate crimes.

Two of my group members and I tirelessly worked on deciding the various data visualizations we could create with our datasets. One focused on showing the gap between personal experiences (as shown through social media posts), reports to the FBI and news coverage. For this visualization, I created a dataset based on a previously published interactive by TAAF and mocked up the interactive element once Skye created the graphics. Another visualization compared the number of news articles on randomly selected crimes, which I also created the dataset for and helped create the visualization on Flourish. We also wanted to perform a sentiment analysis, so I set up a Vader Sentiment Analysis on Python that I found after looking through various data visualization publications.

üôÇ outcome

Overall, I enjoyed searching for and working with datasets, as my group especially needed to make many of our own customizations. In addition, my group initially struggled to find the "story" within the data, so I was challenged to continuously see the big picture and think of data visualization ideas that would best express our story. I saw firsthand how data can be daunting when provided with large datasets but how effective it can be to provide perspective. My group and I came out of this project so proud of ourselves, and we went the extra mile of creating a simple webpage to organize our findings, which I led in the code-front.
