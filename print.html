With a growing interest in design (and due to the very few design-focused courses on campus), I decided early on to join on-campus publications to get more experience in print design. After joining, I grew more confident with drawing illustrations and producing more creative layouts. I was a member of the design staff at North by Northwestern as much as my availability allowed each quarter. The design process for NBN was particularly extensive, as we went through three drafts and spent the last weekend of the quarter doing final checks ‚Äì in which the design and copy are checked by at least three editors for three rounds before being finalized by the Print Managing Editor and Creative Director.

For STITCH and NU Asian, the design process was more autonomous. In addition, the design team did not have as much time with the copy, so there is often a quick turn-around to complete before publication. However, on STITCH, my co-Design editor and I decided to implement the critique process we've experienced in other publications, like NBN, in order to allow for more brainstorming and collaboration within the team while also advocating for a more flexible publication timeline to better serve our designers.
