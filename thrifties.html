Developing an Interactive Story

My team and I reported on and coded an interactive story based on the evolution of thrifting culture, specifically through the lens of the Bay Area.

üôÇ about

In class, we were grouped together based on our skillsets and interests and began with the task of deciding on a story idea. One of my teammates was a thrifting enthusiast, so we went in the direction of focusing on the evolution of thrifting culture in the Bay Area, especially due to the increase of prices caused by the recent trendiness of thrifting.

With my skills in both journalism and web development, I led much of the reporting and coding. As a group, we spent the first few weeks deciding on a story structure and determining our sources. We did on-the-ground reporting at Haight-Ashbury to do background research by speaking with the area's thrift stores and customers. Following, I identified gaps in the reporting and contacted vintage appraisers and Haight-Ashbury locals.

As Sophie and I finished our interviews and determined the story structure, Sophie got to writing and I got to coding based on the drafts we drew up together as a team! Through this project, I was able to follow a story from start to finish and combine both my interests to recognize the value of multidisciplinary skills.
